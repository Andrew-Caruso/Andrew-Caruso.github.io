CERN Summer Student Programme - Intern

June 2022 - August 22

  • 9 week summer internship via the International Research Experience for Students (IRES) at CERN, funded by the National Science Foundation.
  • NA62 Experiment (kaon factory) at CERN
  • Created a python (computer language) program to simulate the CEDAR (differential cherenkov radiation counter) in the NA62 experiment.
  • CERN Technical Report
  • UCR Article on my CERN Internship Experience
  • I had the opportunity to be a part of the International Research Experience for Students (IRES) at CERN internship and the CERN summer student programme for the 2022 summer, coordinated by Professor Philip Rubin. I worked with a postdoctoral researcher, Dr. Chris Parkinson, in the NA62 experiment at CERN, which involves a proton beam collision from the super proton synchrotron (SPS) into a fixed beryllium target in order to search for rare kaon decays. My supervisor, Dr. Parkinson wanted me to simulate the differential Cherenkov radiation counter (CEDAR) near the upstream end of the experiment, in order for him to know how to quickly adjust the components of the CEDAR when the gas, originally nitrogen gas, is changed to hydrogen gas in order to improve efficiency. Essentially, a secondary beam from the upstream collision primarily consists of positive kaons, positive pions, and protons, which travel through the gas-filled CEDAR. Because these charged particles are superluminal within the gas medium, they produce cherenkov radiation off the beam axis and this light is reflected by a spherical mirror, corrected, passed through the ring-like aperture of a diaphragm to filter the out light from other particles save for the kaons and then reflected again into a kaon detector, KTAG, which consists of multipliers to convert the light into a electrical signal. Ultimately the CEDAR/KTAG identifies kaons entering the NA62 experiment at the beginning. I used my previous python skills to build a stand-alone program to replicate expected results by starting with a simplified model of the CEDAR and implementing further complexity into the simulation over time. I then created one technical report at the end of the project, which was made available to other summer students, CERN staff, and the general public on the CERN Document Server. Hopefully, the simulation will be of great use to Dr. Parkison who will use it for the test beam with a hydrogen gas filled CEDAR.
