CERN Summer Student Programme - Intern

June 2022 - August 2022

  • 9 week summer internship via the International Research Experience for Students (IRES) at CERN, funded by the National Science Foundation.
  • NA62 Experiment (kaon factory) at CERN
  • Created a python (computer language) program to simulate the CEDAR (differential cherenkov radiation counter) in the NA62 experiment.
  • CERN Technical Report
  • UCR Article on my CERN Internship Experience
  • This summer I was part of the International Research Experience for Students (IRES) at CERN, coordinated by Professor Philip Rubin from George Mason University. I worked with a postdoctoral researcher, Dr. Chris Parkinson, on the NA62 experiment, which searches for rare kaon decays from proton collisions with a fixed beryllium target. I created a standalone Python program to simulate the differential Cherenkov radiation counter (CEDAR) at the upstream end of the experiment. The CEDAR identifies charged particles by detecting their Cherenkov radiation when they accelerate through nitrogen gas. The purpose was to know how to quickly adjust the components of the CEDAR when the gas is changed to hydrogen gas to potentially improve the efficiency of kaon identification. This will allow the NA62 experiment to better examine rare kaon decays in order to search for lepton flavor/number violations and new particles, such as dark photons. At the end of the internship, I created a technical report which was made available to other CERN staff and the general public on the CERN Document Server.
